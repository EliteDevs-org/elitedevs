A city starts with the ground beneath it

CityBuilder is not a menu skin around a grid. Its terrain system creates the starting conditions: water, grass, sand, and tree tiles appear before a district is placed. That makes the map part of the simulation rather than a blank backdrop.

Terrain

Procedural map

Simplex noise drives the tile map, giving each run a different distribution of land, shoreline, and natural texture without relying on prebuilt level art.

Camera

Built to explore

Pan with WASD, arrow keys, or drag; zoom with the wheel; and use edge scrolling to move through a growing city without losing the overview.

Time

Day becomes night

Dynamic sky and lighting changes make the city feel lived in while keeping the visual stack focused on fast Canvas rendering.

Four kinds of building, one connected model

Residential, commercial, industrial, and civic placements are not just cosmetic categories. They give the simulation its inputs: residents, work, revenue, cost, and the public services that keep the city functioning.

SystemWhat it contributesWhy it matters
ResidentialHomes and population growthTurns placement into a city with residents to support.
CommercialEconomic activity and tax incomeCreates a reason to balance growth against ongoing costs.
IndustrialProduction-oriented land useAdds another development pressure instead of a single all-purpose building type.
CivicSafety, fire, health, education, happinessMakes coverage and city wellbeing visible planning problems.
The intended loop: read the terrain, lay out growth, maintain services, then adjust the budget and tax rate as population and costs begin to pull in different directions.

Designed for direct play

There is no install step, bundler, or opaque runtime between the source and the browser. Open the entry page, choose a building, place it with a left click, and use right click or Escape to cancel. That simplicity is a feature here: the project is an accessible base for experimenting with simulation mechanics.

Power and water gate the city

Every structure declares power and water demand. Each tick compares aggregate supply with demand; a shortfall disables every dependent building. It is a global model rather than a network graph, but it creates a hard planning boundary: an inactive building stops contributing as a productive city asset.

Power

Three generation choices

Coal produces 60 units for $8,000 but adds 30 pollution. Solar produces 35 for $12,000. Wind produces 12 per 1×1 turbine.

Water

Capacity versus footprint

A one-tile tower supplies 30 units for $3,000; the 2×2 plant supplies 60 for $7,000 while consuming four power.

Activation

Both inputs required

A building is active only when powered and watered, tying utility planning directly to occupancy and income.

A monthly budget with five years of memory

Each tick advances a day; 30 days trigger a month and 12 months roll the year forward. Monthly income combines population tax and building income, then subtracts maintenance. The latest 60 months are retained for money, population, happiness, income, and expenses.

Two noise fields shape the map

Two seeded Simplex generators drive terrain. Four-octave fractal Brownian motion supplies elevation; a moisture field breaks up the biome result. Water begins below 0.28, sand runs to 0.33, and trees and rocks use configured densities. Saves retain the seed, so the same terrain is reconstructed before buildings return.

Placement validation

Every footprint checks map bounds, water, occupancy, affordability, and road access before money changes hands.

Coverage decay

Service strength falls with distance into five independent coverage grids.

Pollution field

Industrial assets write into a six-tile pollution radius with linear falloff.

More than 1,100 lines of Canvas rendering

The largest module handles terrain variation, shorelines, roads, structures, previews, overlays, selection, particles, day/night colour, and every building silhouette. Camera culling calculates visible tile bounds so all 3,600 map tiles are not treated equally every frame.

Procedural audio: Web Audio oscillators, generated noise buffers, gain envelopes, and filters create placement, demolition, notification, and milestone sounds without audio assets.

Persistence is already implemented

The game serializes its map seed and buildings, economy, speed, and timestamp to localStorage. Loading regenerates terrain, re-places buildings, restores occupancy and age, rebuilds simulation objects, and rebinds the UI.

BehaviourImplementation
Manual saveWrites a complete JSON snapshot and confirms through notifications.
Automatic saveRuns every six in-game months.
Map restorationRecreates terrain from the seed, then restores structures and runtime values.
Derived systemsCoverage and pollution recompute as structures are restored.

Milestones and disasters

Monthly checks watch population thresholds at 100, 500, 1,000, 5,000, 10,000, 50,000, and 100,000. Separate low-probability checks can trigger fire or earthquake incidents, establishing city risk beyond ordinary budget pressure.

Status

CityBuilder is a substantial static simulation prototype: more than 4,100 lines across eleven modules, 22 placeable definitions, generated terrain, utilities, distance-based services, pollution, happiness composition, a monthly economy, five years of history, milestones, disasters, manual saves, and six-month autosaves. The next work is depth: grid-based utility distribution, fuller disaster consequences, deeper citizen/job modelling, and dense late-game performance validation.

Runtime sequence

What happens during a simulation step

CityBuilder separates real-time drawing from calendar-driven simulation. The animation loop keeps the canvas responsive, while the selected speed determines how quickly game time advances and when economic and population systems receive work.

Data-driven construction

From catalogue entry to working city object

Each of the 22 definitions centralizes price, maintenance, capacity, jobs, utility demand or production, pollution and service effects. Placement turns a definition into a runtime record that can be simulated, rendered, inspected, removed and serialized.

Validation

Input becomes a grid coordinate; occupied, out-of-bounds and unaffordable placement is rejected before cash changes.

Instantiation

The map stores type, tile position and mutable operational fields. The renderer builds matching geometry from that type.

Simulation

Later ticks consume shared properties for housing, jobs, services, pollution, utilities, maintenance and revenue.

Inspection

Panels derive occupancy, capacity, employment, utility availability and economic contribution from live state.

Removal

Bulldozing deletes the object, releases its tile and causes totals and coverage fields to be recalculated.

Serialization

Saves record compact logical state rather than canvas pixels. Loading reconstructs objects and regenerates visuals.

The payoff: adding a building is mainly configuration plus rendering work; economy code does not need another one-off branch for every structure.

Spatial simulation

Services are fields, not city-wide switches

Utilities intentionally use global capacity, but civic services do not. Hospitals, schools, parks, police and fire stations matter most nearby, so CityBuilder models them with grid-aligned coverage maps.

Each source visits tiles inside its radius and deposits a value that falls with distance. Multiple sources can overlap. Pollution follows the same broad method in reverse, letting industry lower nearby quality of life without equally penalizing distant neighborhoods. Residential happiness samples those local conditions alongside global tax, employment, shortage and overcrowding pressures.

Control surface

Camera, tools and feedback form one placement loop

Continuous pointer and camera movement resolve to discrete city edits. Camera panning and zooming remain independent of construction mode, while hover state previews exactly which tile will be affected.

Camera transform

The camera module owns pan, zoom and screen/world conversion, keeping view concerns out of simulation code.

Tile mapping

Pointer coordinates are projected into map space and quantized to the 32-pixel tile grid for preview and placement.

Build preview

Cost, collision, bounds and selected tool produce valid or rejected feedback before a click commits state.

Tool modes

Construction and bulldozing share targeting but dispatch different map operations, avoiding duplicate picking logic.

Diagnosis

Panels, notifications, history charts and resource counters make shortages and stalled growth explainable.

Procedural sound

The 123-line Web Audio module synthesizes interface and construction feedback without external audio assets.
