Paper trading without pretending

Most paper trading systems become optimistic by accident: stale prices, invented fills, and no record of why an order executed. paperpoly takes the opposite position. If it cannot obtain live data it returns an explicit error; if a fill happens, it carries the live-book evidence that justified it; and if a paper order would create an unrealistic result, the system calls that out.

Compatibility

Drop in, don’t rewrite

Existing Polymarket clients can be aimed at paperpoly’s local hosts rather than the production endpoints. The official py-clob-client has been exercised through auth, order, and cancel flow with only the host URL changed.

Live data

Real market context

Market data passes through from the real APIs at request time. The goal is to keep the conditions a strategy sees grounded in the market that actually exists.

Simulation

Orders stay local

Accounts, positions, balances, lifecycle events, and settlement are simulated locally. State-changing traffic is intercepted rather than trusted not to escape.

Execution realism is the product

paperpoly does more than mirror a quote and say an order filled. Taker orders consume live-book depth level by level; fee logic uses the live market schedule; and resting orders begin with a queue position based on the observed book rather than a flattering assumption.

Order behaviourHow paperpoly handles itWhat it prevents
FOK / FAK market ordersWalk the live order book level by level, with configurable decision-to-execution latency and fresh-book requirements.Fills at a single imaginary price when depth is thin.
GTC / GTD resting ordersStart with queue position from the live book and fill only as observed real trades consume depth ahead.Paper orders that get priority they could never have had.
FeesRead market fee schedules and apply the rate curve and taker-only rule.Systematic overcharging or undercharging from a generic constant.
SettlementResolve positions only when the real API can verify market resolution, keeping the evidence.Premature or fabricated outcomes.
The honest limitation: paper orders have no market impact. Rather than hide that, paperpoly flags fills that consume a large share of visible depth so the strategy can discount those results.

Safety is enforced by architecture

The core rule is simple: a paper-trading gateway must never become a path to placing a real order. paperpoly separates real read traffic from local simulated state, and treats degraded upstream data as a reason to pause rather than invent a convenient answer.

No upstream writes

Only read-style requests are forwarded. State-changing paths are structurally intercepted so paper activity cannot touch the exchange.

No ghost data

Failed live fetches return explicit errors. A stale WebSocket mirror pauses fills instead of making up a price or backfilling a result.

Evidence retained

Every fill stores its relevant book snapshot, requests are logged end to end, and observed API anomalies accumulate into an empirical limits ledger.

paperpoly emulates the main HTTP and WebSocket surfaces a client already expects, then routes reads to the appropriate upstream source and routes all trade state into its local simulation engine.

A taker order crosses twelve explicit boundaries

Order placement is the core trust path. It validates the request, models latency, fetches current external evidence, applies real order semantics, performs accounting under a per-account lock, stores evidence, and only then emits a compatible response.

StepWhat happens
1 · L2 identityDeterministic paper credentials resolve to a local account; signatures are recorded but intentionally not cryptographically enforced.
2 · Shape validationSide, size, price, order type, tick alignment, duplicate id, expiration, and supported time-in-force are checked with real-shaped errors.
3 · Account serializationThe entire asynchronous admission path runs under a per-address queue so two orders cannot spend the same cash or shares.
4 · LatencyDefault 100 ms is waited before the book request, allowing the market to move during decision-to-execution time.
5 · Fresh bookA live REST book is fetched after latency and rejected if unavailable or outside the configured freshness bound.
6 · Book walkBUY consumes asks low-to-high; SELL consumes bids high-to-low; no level crosses the order’s limit.
7 · OverlayRecently consumed paper liquidity is subtracted for ten seconds so rapid local orders cannot eat the same visible size twice.
8 · Order semanticsFOK requires full size, FAK keeps the available part, GTC/GTD execute the marketable part and rest the remainder.
9 · Fee evidenceGamma’s live fee schedule is validated and applied per consumed level.
10 · Commit recheckCash, inventory, and commitments are verified again at the database write boundary.
11 · AccountingUSDC, positions, cost basis, realized PnL, orders, and trade rows update atomically.
12 · Evidence and eventsThe justifying book snapshot, fill levels, depth share, fee schedule, request id, order event, and lifecycle task are stored/emitted.
Market BUY distinction: like the real client, a market BUY targets dollars while other paths target shares. The engine’s parsing and book walking preserve that difference.

Visible depth is measured twice: for execution and for honesty

The book walk derives the average price and slippage from real levels. Separately, the engine records total marketable depth at the decision point and what share the paper fill consumed. That second measure is crucial because a technically executable paper fill may still be too large to believe without modelling market impact.

Level-by-level fills

Each consumed price and size is retained, so nonlinear fees and slippage can be recomputed rather than inferred from an average.

20% depth flag

The realism report marks any fill using more than one fifth of visible marketable depth as impact-sensitive.

Verbatim books

Client /book responses are never doctored to hide locally consumed liquidity; only the internal overlay prevents double-use.

FOK integrity

If the eligible live levels cannot satisfy the complete size inside the limit, nothing is booked.

FAK integrity

Only visible eligible size fills; the remainder is cancelled rather than imagined.

Freshness proof

Every accepted decision carries its real fetch timestamp and can be rejected by maxBookAgeMsForFill.

The maker model starts behind the real queue

A resting GTC/GTD order begins with queueAhead equal to the size already quoted at its exact price in the live mirror. From there, only observed feed events can move it toward a fill.

Observed eventQueue / fill behaviour
Trade at the paper order’s priceConsumes queue ahead first; only leftover trade volume reaches the paper order.
Several paper orders at one priceOne real trade is one shared budget, applied FIFO across local orders.
Trade through the paper priceFills the remaining order because price priority implies its level had to trade first.
Real cancel at the priceCan only clamp queue ahead down to observed size; it is never assumed to have been ahead.
Feed disconnect / resync crossingMay infer a fill at the limit, but tags it book_crossed_at_resync for separate reporting.
Process restartLive orders reload from SQLite and re-arm only after a fresh mirror snapshot.
GTD deadlineExpiration task cancels the remaining quantity when its timestamp arrives.
Conservative by design: public data cannot reveal the true private queue. paperpoly chooses assumptions that delay or reduce fills rather than make a strategy look better.

The fee model was audited against the live schedule

The fee is computed separately for every consumed level because the curve is nonlinear: size × rate × (p × (1 − p))^exponent. At a 0.50 price, 100 crypto shares at a 0.07 rate cost $1.75; sports at 0.05 cost $1.25; politics at 0.04 cost $1.00—matching the published caps.

Ceiling is not rate

CLOB’s base_fee: 1000 is the maximum signed fee, not a 10% trading charge. The earlier interpretation overcharged by 1.4×–2.5× and is documented.

Live schedule

Rate, exponent, taker-only rule, type, and rebate metadata come from the Gamma market object and are cached for one hour by default.

Fail loud

If the fee-rate signal or usable schedule is unavailable, the order returns 503 instead of assuming zero.

USDC accounting

BUY adds fee to cash cost and cost basis; SELL subtracts it from proceeds and realized PnL.

Maker exemption

Observed schedules are taker-only, so resting fills do not fetch or charge a fee.

Reproducible modes

schedule, legacy flat-bps, and zero modes preserve old-result reproducibility while defaulting to the audited model.

Trades move through a visible settlement lifecycle

A successful immediate fill begins at MATCHED, then the lifecycle scheduler advances it through MINED and CONFIRMED after configurable delays. User-channel clients receive the corresponding order and trade events, while the database remains the source of truth for restarts.

Resolution requires live evidence

A background monitor runs every 60 seconds by default and also activates when an open position loses its price mark or an order encounters a dead book. Primary evidence is the CLOB market token’s winner flag; the engine also records the supporting market response.

OutcomeAccounting
Winning tokenPosition pays $1 per share into cash and records settlement PnL.
Losing tokenPosition settles at $0 and records the loss against cost basis.
Not definitively resolvedState remains open; dead marks do not silently become zero.
Resolved market with resting ordersRelated live orders are cancelled and mirror tracking can stop.
Data API activitySettlement appears as a REDEEM row alongside TRADE activity.

SQLite is the paper exchange ledger

One WAL-mode database, implemented with Node’s built-in node:sqlite, stores accounts, deterministic API keys, orders and commitments, trades, positions, book snapshots, settlements, and notifications. Money and share quantities use integer micro-units to avoid floating-point drift in the accounting core.

Atomic balance changes

Admission is serialized per account and rechecked inside the write transaction.

Position cost basis

Buys update weighted average cost including fees; sells update realized PnL.

Order commitments

Resting BUY cash and SELL inventory remain unavailable to competing orders.

Cursor pagination

Order and trade history follows the client-facing pagination shapes.

Idempotent migrations

Schema evolution is covered by database tests rather than manual setup assumptions.

Reset boundary

Deleting the SQLite file resets paper state; live market data is never a stored substitute.

Accounts can move without losing their audit trail

GET /paperpoly/export produces a versioned account dump containing account data, keys, orders, trades, positions, settlements, notifications, and the book snapshots referenced by those trades. POST /paperpoly/import validates ownership across every row, replaces that account atomically, remaps snapshot ids, re-arms live orders, and resumes incomplete lifecycles.

Why snapshots are inlined: an exported result should remain audit-complete when moved to another installation, rather than retaining broken references into the old database.

One gateway can supervise hundreds of paper accounts

A naïve supervisor multiplies every position mark by accounts × held tokens. Sixty accounts with eight positions would create roughly 480 upstream calls for one fleet view. paperpoly provides two different mechanisms because fleet state and live pricing are different problems.

MechanismGuarantee
Bulk positionsUp to 500 addresses; cash, positions, average price, realized PnL, and open orders directly from SQLite with zero upstream calls and deliberately no marks.
Single-flight GETsConcurrent internal requests for the same upstream path await one in-flight response; this is deduplication, not caching.
Passthrough isolationClient proxy requests never coalesce because their headers and provenance are their own.
Operational statsCounts requests, coalesced calls, throttle waits, refused load, real 429s, row counts, snapshot bytes, and effective logging config.
Example fleetSixty paper bots share one market scan; a complete cycle costs seven upstream calls whether twelve or sixty bots run.

Auditability has an explicit disk budget

The default audit profile keeps every event and full snapshot forever. balanced and lean trade replayability and write volume for bounded storage while retaining numerical realism metrics on the trade rows.

ProfileEventsFill evidenceRetentionRough cost
AuditFull, synchronousComplete bookForever~100 MB/day + ~4 KB/fill
BalancedStandard, 1 s bufferedConsumed + nearby ladder14 days / 512 MB~10 MB/day gzipped + ~600 B/fill
LeanMinimal, 5 s bufferedTop-of-book + hash3 days / 64 MB; snapshots 2 days~2 MB/day gzipped + ~200 B/fill
Retention stays honest: trade rows distinguish evidence never stored from evidence later pruned. The report counts fully replayable, compact, hash-only, and missing snapshots. The janitor never removes evidence from a trade still moving to CONFIRMED, and the observed-limits ledger is never pruned.

The realism report grades the result, not the strategy

npm run report generates a Markdown audit over the database: per-fill slippage against the justifying book, marketable depth consumed, maker evidence type, inferred resync fills, fee assumptions, settlement PnL, snapshot coverage, and standing limitations. It does not claim whether a strategy is good; it tells the reader how much confidence to place in the paper execution.

The limitations are part of the interface

LimitationHow paperpoly handles it
No market impactRecords depth share and flags large fills instead of inventing reactions from other traders.
Public queue ambiguityUses conservative queue estimates and separates observed from inferred evidence.
Feed gapsPauses stale fills; resync crossings are tagged rather than presented as ordinary trades.
Signed fee ceilingRecords but does not yet enforce the signer’s feeRateBps acceptance rule.
Flagged-market delayDoes not yet auto-detect and apply Polymarket’s extra 250 ms taker delay.
Unsupported mechanicsPost-only orders, neg-risk conversions, RTDS, and real liquidity rewards remain outside v1.
Simulated chainTransaction hashes and settlement lifecycle are compatible shapes, not Polygon transactions.

Status

paperpoly is feature-complete for its first milestone: gateway compatibility, simulated market and resting orders, WebSocket lifecycle events, settlement, and realism reporting are in place.
